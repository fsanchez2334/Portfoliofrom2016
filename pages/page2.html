Chains by Laurie Halse Anderson

In this book, the willpower of a young girl is put to the test during the events of the American Revolution. This girl is named Isabela, and she is thirteen years old who, along with her sister, are bought by two masters during a slave market. Upon arriving in a new place with her wealthy masters, Isabel sees the internal division of the Patriots and the Loyalists. Eventually, this road crosses her path with another slave boy, Curzon, and both are pitted against multiple dangerous odds during the Revolution. Both will come to realize the importance of friendship in the presence of chaos.

The Count of Monte Cristo by Alexandre Dumas

In this book, a young and innovative sailor named Edmond Dantes is worngly jailed for being a Bonapartist traitor during the reign of Napoleon.Depite of him being innocent, Edmond remains for apporximately fourteen years in the Chateau d'If, a prison for people with petty crimes. After being on the verge of suicide, he befriends a fellow cell mate who was planning his freedom. After they soon become friends, Edmond receives extensive education in science, culture, and science. Nevertheless, in the following days, his cell mate known as "The Mad Priest" grows sick and weary, unable to escape, and eventualky dies. Honoring the legacy of his mentor, Edmond escapes the prison and seeks to help his long lost allies from the past, after ineriting the fortune of "The Mad Priest", located on the island of Monte Cristo, and to Edmond's surprise, the island had an extensive amountof jewels and gold. Soon after, he assumesthe identity of "The Count of Monte Cristo", and goes out to help his old allies from the past with this wealth. However, after helping his friends, he goes out in a spirt of vengeance to exact revenge on the conspirators involved in his imprisonment. Overall, after a long wait and being locked away, "The Count of Monte Cristo" carries out the epitome of poetic justice.

To Kill a Mockingbird

The unforgettable novel of a childhood in a sleepy Southern town and the crisis of conscience that rocked it, To Kill a Mockingbird takes readersto the roots of human behavior-to innocence and experience, kindness and cruelty, love and hatred, humor and pathos. Harper Lee always considered her book to be a simple love story;today, it is regarded as a masterpiece of American literature.

In Cold Blood by Truman Capote

On November 15,1959, in the small town of Holcomb, Kansas, four members of the Clutter family were savagely murdered by blasts from a shotgun held a few inches from their faces. There was no apparent motive for the crime, and there were almost no clues.As Truman Capote reconstructs the murder and the investigation that led to the capture, trial, and execution of the killers, he generates both mesmorizing suspense and astonishing empathy.
